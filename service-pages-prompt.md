## Individual Service Pages

### 1. Bone Grafting
**URL:** `/services/bone-grafting`
**Hero subheadline:** Restoring jawbone density for a strong foundation for dental implants and long-lasting results.
**What is Bone Grafting?**
Bone grafting is a surgical procedure that rebuilds or restores bone tissue in the jaw. It's commonly performed before dental implant placement when a patient's jawbone has deteriorated due to tooth loss, periodontal disease, or injury. The procedure uses natural or synthetic bone material to encourage new bone growth, creating a stable foundation for implants.
**Key Benefits:**
- Creates a stable foundation for dental implants
- Prevents further bone loss after tooth extraction
- Enables patients who were previously not candidates for implants to receive them
- Promotes natural bone regeneration over time
**Who is a candidate:**
- Patients with insufficient jawbone density for implants
- Those who have experienced bone loss from periodontal disease
- Patients needing tooth replacement who lack adequate bone structure
- People seeking dental implants but told they need bone reconstruction first

### 3. Dental Implants
**URL:** `/services/dental-implants`
**Hero subheadline:** Permanent tooth replacement that looks, feels, and functions like natural teeth — with significant savings.
**What are Dental Implants?**
Dental implants are titanium posts surgically placed into the jawbone to serve as artificial tooth roots. Once the implant integrates with the bone (a process called osseointegration), a custom-made crown, bridge, or denture is attached to restore full function and aesthetics. Dental implants are the gold standard for tooth replacement because they preserve jawbone health, prevent adjacent teeth from shifting, and can last a lifetime with proper care.
**Key Benefits:**
- Permanent, stable solution that functions like natural teeth
- Preserves jawbone health and prevents bone loss
- No need to alter adjacent healthy teeth
- Can last a lifetime with proper oral hygiene
**Who is a candidate:**
- Adults missing one or more teeth
- Patients with sufficient jawbone density (or those who can receive bone grafting)
- Those seeking a permanent alternative to bridges or dentures
- Non-smokers or those willing to quit during the healing process

**#### All-on-4®/All-on-5®/All-on-6®/All-on-8® Sub-Sections**
These are subsections within the Dental Implants page (NOT separate pages).

**All-on-4®:**
A full-arch restoration supported by just 4 strategically placed implants. This technique is ideal for patients missing all teeth in an arch and offers a permanent, non-removable solution with a faster healing time. The implants are angled to maximize bone contact, often eliminating the need for bone grafting.

**All-on-5®:**
Similar to All-on-4 but with 5 implants for added stability and support. The extra implant provides greater distribution of bite force, making it suitable for patients with slightly higher bite requirements or softer bone density.

**All-on-6®:**
A full-arch prosthesis supported by 6 implants, offering maximum stability and load distribution. This option is ideal for patients with good bone density who want the most secure, long-lasting full-arch solution.

**All-on-8®:**
The most comprehensive full-arch solution, using 8 implants for ultimate strength and durability. Best suited for patients with excellent bone quality who want the most secure prosthetic possible.

### 5. Endodontic Procedures
**URL:** `/services/endodontic-procedures`
**Hero subheadline:** Root canal treatment and other endodontic procedures performed by specialists using advanced technology for painless, effective results.
**What are Endodontic Procedures?**
Endodontic procedures treat the inside of the tooth, most commonly through root canal treatment. When the pulp inside a tooth becomes infected or inflamed due to deep decay, cracks, or trauma, a root canal removes the infected tissue, cleans the canal, and seals it to prevent further infection. Our specialists use advanced rotary instruments and digital imaging to perform root canals with precision and minimal discomfort.
**Key Benefits:**
- Save your natural tooth rather than extracting it
- Performed by endodontic specialists with advanced training
- Modern technology for faster, more comfortable treatment
- Gentle anesthesia and sedation options available
**Who is a candidate:**
- Patients experiencing tooth pain, sensitivity to hot or cold, or swelling
- Those with deep decay or a cracked tooth
- Patients whose dentist has recommended root canal treatment
- Anyone seeking to save a tooth rather than have it extracted

### 7. Restorative Dentistry
**URL:** `/services/restorative-dentistry`
**Hero subheadline:** Crowns, bridges, inlays, and onlays that restore damaged teeth to their natural function and appearance.
**What is Restorative Dentistry?**
Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing or replacing damaged, decayed, or missing teeth to restore both function and appearance. Using materials like porcelain, zirconia, and composite resin, our specialists create crowns, bridges, inlays, and onlays that blend seamlessly with your natural teeth. We prioritize treatments that preserve as much of your natural tooth structure as possible.
**Key Benefits:**
- Natural-looking results that match your existing teeth
- Durable materials designed to withstand normal biting and chewing
- Digital imaging and CAD/CAM technology for precise fit
- Treatments that preserve healthy tooth structure whenever possible
**Who is a candidate:**
- Patients with cracked, worn, or damaged teeth
- Those with large fillings that need replacement
- People missing one or more teeth (bridges)
- Anyone seeking to restore the function and appearance of damaged teeth

### 8. Sedation Dentistry
**URL:** `/services/sedation-dentistry`

### 10. Wisdom Teeth
**URL:** `/services/wisdom-teeth`
**Hero subheadline:** Safe, comfortable wisdom tooth extraction by experienced oral surgeons in our state-of-the-art surgical facility.
**What is Wisdom Teeth Removal?**
Wisdom teeth, or third molars, typically emerge in the late teens or early twenties. When they become impacted (unable to fully emerge), grow at an angle, or cause crowding, extraction is recommended. Our oral surgeons perform wisdom tooth extractions using advanced techniques and sedation options to ensure a comfortable experience with minimal recovery time.
**Key Benefits:**
- Oral surgeons with extensive experience in complex extractions
- Digital imaging to assess root development and impacted positioning
- Sedation options for maximum comfort during the procedure
- Post-operative care instructions and follow-up for smooth recovery
**Who is a candidate:**
- Teenagers or young adults whose wisdom teeth are emerging
- Patients with impacted or partially erupted wisdom teeth
- Those experiencing pain, swelling, or infection related to wisdom teeth
- Anyone whose dentist has recommended wisdom tooth extraction
